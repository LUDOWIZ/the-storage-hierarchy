# **The Storage Hierarchy** 🌍

> **From Bits to Exabytes:** Visualizing the scale, geography, and technology of modern cloud infrastructure.

## ✨ The Vision
Ever wondered where your **5 TB Google Drive** actually *lives*? To most of us, "The Cloud" is just a loading bar or a number on a screen. In reality, it is a massive physical footprint consisting of silicon, spinning disks, deep-sea fiber cables, and concrete fortresses.

This project aims to map the journey from the smallest unit of data — the **Bit** — to the **Exabyte-scale** infrastructure that powers our global civilization.

---

## 🗺️ The Hierarchy of Scale
To understand the cloud, we have to understand the scale. We are mapping the world in these steps:

1.  **The Micro (The Bit):** The atomic level. A single pulse of electricity.
2.  **The Personal (The Terabyte):** Your 5 TB slice. If this were physical paper, it would fill a small library.
3.  **The Local (The Data Center):** Where your files live. A building consuming as much power as a small town.
4.  **The Global (The Exabyte):** The total sum of human knowledge, distributed across continents via undersea cables.

---

## 📍 Why This Project?
The cloud isn't magic; it's **geography** and **physics**. 
- **Geography:** Why is your data stored in Luleå (Sweden) or Saint-Ghislain (Belgium)? (Hint: It's about the cold air and cheap power).
- **Physics:** How do we move 5 TB of photos across the Atlantic Ocean in milliseconds?

We want to visualize this "invisible" infrastructure through data, maps, and clear comparisons.
